Mass balance and pressure equation¶

Introduction¶

In this part, we consider to discretise

\[\der{u_i}{x_i} = 0\]

at cell center \(\left( \pic, \pjc \right)\).

Also the discrete form of the pressure equation

\[\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x_i \partial x_i} = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \der{u_i^*}{x_i}\]

is derived.

Discrete incompressibility constraint¶

The incompressibility constraint at cell center \(\left( \pic, \pjc \right)\) is discretised as

\[\dder{\ux}{x} + \dder{\uy}{y} = 0.\]
Derivations

As being discussed in the grid arrangement, in this project, cell center (where pressure and temperature are located) lies in the middle of surrounding cell faces (where velocities are located), i.e.,

\[\begin{split}\vat{x}{\pic} & = \frac{1}{2} \vat{x}{\pip} + \frac{1}{2} \vat{x}{\pim}, \\ \vat{y}{\pjc} & = \frac{1}{2} \vat{y}{\pjp} + \frac{1}{2} \vat{y}{\pjm}.\end{split}\]

Since velocities are defined at cell face, it is natural to define the incompressibility constraint at cell center, i.e.,

\[\der{u_i}{x_i} \left( x, y \right) \approx \vat{\dder{u_i}{x_i}}{\pic,\pjc} = \frac{\vat{\ux}{\pip} - \vat{\ux}{\pim}}{\vat{x}{\pip} - \vat{x}{\pim}} + \frac{\vat{\uy}{\pjp} - \vat{\uy}{\pjm}}{\vat{y}{\pjp} - \vat{y}{\pjm}} = 0,\]

so that it is isotropic with respect to each velocity component. In other words, this continuity is only valid at each cell center, which is different from continuous domain where all infinitesimal control volumes obey it.

Discrete pressure equation¶

Pressure equation at \(\left( \pic, \pjc \right)\) should be discretised as

\[\dder{}{x} \left( \dder{\psi}{x} \right) + \dder{}{y} \left( \dder{\psi}{y} \right) = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \left( \dder{\ux^*}{x} + \dder{\uy^*}{y} \right).\]
Derivations

As being discussed in the temporal discretisation, the continuity is enforced by

\[u_i^{n+1} = u_i^{*} - \der{\psi}{x_i} \Delta t,\]

or written explicitly at each cell face:

\[\begin{split}\vat{\ux^{n+1}}{\pip} &= \vat{\ux^*}{\pip} - \vat{\der{\psi}{x}}{\pip} \Delta t, \\ \vat{\ux^{n+1}}{\pim} &= \vat{\ux^*}{\pim} - \vat{\der{\psi}{x}}{\pim} \Delta t, \\ \vat{\uy^{n+1}}{\pjp} &= \vat{\uy^*}{\pjp} - \vat{\der{\psi}{y}}{\pjp} \Delta t, \\ \vat{\uy^{n+1}}{\pjm} &= \vat{\uy^*}{\pjm} - \vat{\der{\psi}{y}}{\pjm} \Delta t,\end{split}\]

where \(\psi\) is a scalar potential. Assigning this relation to the above discrete incompressibility constraint yields

\[\frac{ \vat{\der{\psi}{x}}{\pip} - \vat{\der{\psi}{x}}{\pim} }{ \vat{x}{\pip} - \vat{x}{\pim} } + \frac{ \vat{\der{\psi}{y}}{\pjp} - \vat{\der{\psi}{y}}{\pjm} }{ \vat{y}{\pjp} - \vat{y}{\pjm} } = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \frac{\vat{\ux^*}{\pip} - \vat{\ux^*}{\pim}}{\vat{x}{\pip} - \vat{x}{\pim}} + \frac{\vat{\uy^*}{\pjp} - \vat{\uy^*}{\pjm}}{\vat{y}{\pjp} - \vat{y}{\pjm}},\]

which is the discrete pressure equation

\[\frac{\delta^2 \psi}{\delta x_i \delta x_i} = \frac{1}{\Delta t} \dder{u_i^*}{x_i}.\]

Warning

When the grid sizes are non-uniform, the above discretisation is different from what can be derived using the naive Taylor series expansion of

\[\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x_i \partial x_i}.\]

In other words, if one discretises the pressure equation naively using Taylor series, the mass balance is violated. To avoid this, one needs to design the domain differently, which is out of focus in this project.
